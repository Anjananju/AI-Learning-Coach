Chapter 1 – Introduction to Artificial Intelligence


1. What is Artificial Intelligence?
Definition
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that focuses on building systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence.
These tasks include:
•	Learning 
•	Reasoning 
•	Decision making 
•	Understanding language 
•	Recognizing images 
•	Solving problems 
In simple words,
AI is the ability of a computer to imitate human intelligence.
 
Examples of AI
Everyday examples include:
•	ChatGPT 
•	Siri 
•	Google Assistant 
•	Netflix Recommendations 
•	Amazon Product Recommendations 
•	Face Unlock 
•	Spam Email Detection 
•	Self-driving Cars 
 
Goals of AI
The primary goal of AI is to create systems that can:
•	Learn from data 
•	Make intelligent decisions 
•	Adapt to new situations 
•	Solve complex problems automatically 
 
Types of AI
AI can be classified into three categories.
1. Narrow AI
Designed to perform a single task.
Examples:
•	ChatGPT 
•	Google Translate 
•	Face Recognition 
Most AI systems today belong to this category.
 
2. General AI (AGI)
A machine capable of performing any intellectual task that a human can do.
Characteristics:
•	Can learn any subject 
•	Can reason 
•	Can adapt independently 
AGI does not yet exist.
 
3. Super AI
A hypothetical AI that surpasses human intelligence in every field.
This is currently theoretical.

Artificial Intelligence
│
├── Machine Learning
│

│     ├── Unsupervised Learning

│
├── Deep Learning
│

This hierarchy is one of the most important concepts to remember.
 
Relationship Between AI, ML, DL and Generative AI
Artificial Intelligence is the broadest field.
Machine Learning is a subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data instead of following manually written rules.
Deep Learning is a subset of Machine Learning that uses neural networks with multiple layers.
Generative AI is a branch of AI that creates new content such as text, images, videos, music, and code.
 
Real-Life Analogy
Imagine teaching a child.
Rule-based programming:
IF it has feathers
THEN Bird
Machine Learning:
The child observes thousands of birds and learns the pattern.
Deep Learning:
The child automatically discovers the important features without being explicitly told.
Generative AI:
The child can now draw a completely new bird that has never been seen before.
 
Important Interview Question
Q: Is every AI system based on Machine Learning?
Answer:
No.
Many traditional AI systems are rule-based and do not use Machine Learning.
Machine Learning is only one approach within Artificial Intelligence.
 
Common Mistakes
❌ AI = Machine Learning
✔ Machine Learning is a subset of AI.
 
❌ Deep Learning = AI
✔ Deep Learning is a subset of Machine Learning.
 
❌ ChatGPT = Artificial Intelligence
✔ ChatGPT is an application powered by a Large Language Model, which is part of Generative AI.
 
Key Takeaways
•	AI is the broadest field. 
•	Machine Learning is a subset of AI. 
•	Deep Learning is a subset of Machine Learning. 
•	Generative AI creates new content. 
•	Most AI today is Narrow AI.
